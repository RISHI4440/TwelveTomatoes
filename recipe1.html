Battered or Crumbed Fried Fish Sandwiches
Rating: 4.5/5

Battered or Crumbed Fried Fish Sandwiches

Active Time 30 minutes

Total Time 1/2 hours plus cooling

When it comes to the fillets in fried fish sandwiches, there is always the question. Why make a decision? To get a rich, maximal crunch, we went with both.

Because of the flour in the recipe, we were able to create a creamy batter that will adhere to the fillets. Next, using an air fryer, we coated the fillets in panko to provide a ton of crunch to each bite. Consequently, we get the best of both worlds—a coating that is rich but still crunchy.

You may up the ante by dressing each sandwich with a slice of sharp American-style cheddar cheese or by dusting the crispy fish fillets with malt vinegar before assembling the sandwiches.

This dish is taken from Bruce Weinstein and Mark Scarbrough's book, "The Look and Cook Air Fryer Bible."

What you’ll need

  • 2 cups (100 g) plain panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1⁄4 cup regular or low-fat mayonnaise (do not use fat-free)
  • 1 Tbsp. Dijon mustard
  • 1 Tbsp. (8 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1⁄2 tsp. mild paprika
  • 1⁄2 tsp. onion powder
  • 1⁄2 tsp. tablespoon salt
  • 1⁄2 tsp. ground black pepper
  • Four 4- to 6-oz. (115- to 140-g) cod fillet sections (thawed if frozen)
  • Nonstick spray
  • 4 white burger buns, preferably potato buns
  • Tartar sauce, as a condiment
