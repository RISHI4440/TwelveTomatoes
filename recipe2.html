Gluten Free Pumpkin Pie

Active Time 25 minutes

Total Time 1 hours plus cooling

This recipe for gluten-free pumpkin pie has it all: a tasty crust, a spiced pumpkin filling, and simple stages that can be completed several days ahead of time. The filling has an autumnal flavor thanks to the ginger and ground cinnamon, but you may adjust the recipe by adding your preferred pumpkin pie spices, such as ground cardamom or allspice, or a dash of vanilla extract.

The gluten-free pie crust recipe is especially revelatory for people who have previously been burned by gluten-free baking. A tiny quantity of an all-purpose gluten-free flour blend is included in the mixture, but store-bought or homemade oat flour—made by pulverizing old-fashioned oats in a food processor—provides the majority of the flour blend's nutty punch.

Serve this just like a regular pumpkin pie, or top each slice with a spoonful of whipped cream.For a Thanksgiving dessert mashup, sprinkle with flaky sea salt or a handful of candied brown sugar pecans.

You can prepare the dough up to three days prior to baking (refrigerate till use). This will cut down on prep time.

What you’ll need

  • 8–12 servings Crust
  • Gluten-free all-purpose flour (for surface)
  • 1 disk Gluten-Free Pie Crust plus 1 optional disk for garnish
  • Filling and assembly
  • 2 large eggs plus 1 large egg yolk, divided
  • 1 cup dark-amber maple syrup
  • 2 cups canned pumpkin purée
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ⅔ cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1 tsp. ground ginger
  • ½ tsp. Diamond Crystal or ¼ tsp. Morton kosher salt
