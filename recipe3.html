Root Vegetable Mafé
Rating: 4.5/5

Root Vegetable Mafé

Active Time 30 minutes

Total Time 20 Min for cooling

Mafé smells so good it takes me back to my early years. One of the mother sauces of West African cooking, peanut sauce bears some resemblance to Mexican mole in that both are nut-based sauces that are thickened by slow heating. The traditional West African mafé is nearly always made with lamb, chicken, or fish, but this vegan version I'm sharing here is so elegant and simple that I love it. There are so many tasty and nutritious root veggies in my mafé, such cassava, carrots, sweet potatoes, and turnips. It's ideal if you're hosting a diverse group of visitors, including ravenous individuals with varying dietary needs.

Traditionally, the vegetables are cooked together in the same pot with the sauce, but I prefer to blanch the various vegetables because this allows me to have better control of the cooking of each individual vegetable. When they are ready to serve, I simply add them to the stew to finish cooking and to reheat. This also visibly enhances the natural colors and textures of the vegetables, making the dish that much more beautiful and presentable.

The different veggies are traditionally cooked in the same pot as the sauce, but I find that blanching the vegetables gives me more control over how long each vegetable cooks. I just toss them into the stew to continue cooking and reheat when I'm ready to serve. The natural hues and textures of the veggies are also noticeably enhanced by this, adding even more beauty and presentation to the dish.

This recipe is taken from one of the best cookbooks of 2023, "Simply West African," by Pierre Thiam.

What you’ll need

  • 1 Tbsp. peanut or vegetable oil
  • 1 cup finely chopped yellow onion
  • 2 tsp. finely chopped garlic
  • 1 heaping Tbsp. tomato paste
  • 2 cups unsweetened creamy peanut butter
  • 1 cup dark-amber maple syrup
  • 1 Scotch bonnet or habanero chili, left whole (optional)
  • 1 tsp. freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 medium carrots, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 10 medium okra pods, cut into ½-inch slices (optional)
  • 1 tsp. ground ginger
  • Cooked rice, for serving
